1918

August 4th - Australian Prime MinisterAndrew Fisher: "Australia will support Great Britain 'to the last man and the last shilling".

August 5th - German Steamer SS Pfalz surrenders after being fired on by Fort Nepean,south of Melbourne, Australia.

10th Aug - Germans living in Australia declared ‘enemy aliens’ and required to report to their nearest police station.

Aug 13th - The Australian Red Cross is established to raise funds to purchase supplies for Australian service personnel overseas.

Sep 17th - The Australian Naval and Military Expeditionary Force takes possession of German New Guinea at Toma.

Nov 1st - The first AIF contingent sailsfrom Albany, WA, to Egypt with around 30,000 troops and 14,873 horses.

Nov 9th - HMAS Sydney destroys the German cruiser Emden in battle near the Cocos Islands.

Dec 3rd - Australian troops arrive in Egypt and set up camp at Mena, near Cairo.

Jan 15th - The second Australian convoy arrives in Egypt. The second Australian convoy arrives in Egypt.

8th Feb - HMAS Australia, Sydney and Melbourne assist with the naval blockade of Germany in the North Sea.

25th Apr - Australian, New Zealand, British and French troops land at Gallipoli.

15th May - Major General William T Bridges, first commander of the AIF, is hit by a sniper's bullet at Gallipoli and dies three days.

24th May - There is a formal truce on Gallipoli to bury the bodies of Turkish and ANZAC soldiers.

1st Jun - Australian recruitment increases as state-based Parliamentary recruiting committees become active in towns and shires.

6th Aug - Battle of Lone Pine. Four days with over 2000 Australian casualties. Seven Australians are awarded the Victoria Cross.

7th Aug - Australian Light Horsemen attack Turkish trenches at the Nek at 4.30 am.

29th Aug - Anzacs abandon further Gallipoli offensives and a stalemate begins.

27th Oct - Billy Hughes becomes Prime Minister, replacing Andrew Fisher who resigns due to ill health and party divisions.

18th Dec - 20,000 Allied troops are evacuated off Gallipoli where 8709 had died and 26,111 were wounded.

Mar 1st - The AIF is expanded from two to five infantry divisions which progressively sail to France.

Apr 25th - This day is officially named ANZAC Day. Commemorative services are held across the Commonwealth.

July 1st - The battle of the Somme begins. Fought in northern France, it was one of the bloodiest of World War One.

July 19th - Battle of Fromelles. The first major battle for Australian troops on the Western Front, there are 5533 casualties in 24 hours.

Jul 23rd - During the battle of the Somme Australians capture Pozières , resulting in 23,000 casualties with 6741 killed.

4th Aug - The 1st and 2nd Australian Light Horse Brigades defend the Suez Canal from Turkey in the Battle of Romani, Egypt.

24th Aug - The Australian Comforts Fund is officially inaugurated to collect money to send parcels to service personnel.

28th Oct - First Conscription Referendum results: – for 1,087,557, – against 1,160,033.

18th Nov - Australian troops man trenches on the Western Front. Winter temperatures drop to -20 degrees. Some found frozen to death.

26th Mar - Australian forces battle to recapture the village of Lagnicourt in Northern France from German forces.

11 Apr - First battle of Bullecourt in France. Two brigades of the Allied 4th Division suffer over 3300 casualties, 1170 are taken prisoner.

May 3rd - Second Battle of Bullecourt. After weeks of intensive fighting, the Germans withdraw from the remnants of the village.

7th Jun - Anzac troops capture the high ground south of Ypres after Allied tunnellers detonate mines under German positions.

31st Jul -Third Battle of Ypres begins. Australian soldiers in action at Menin Road, Glencorse Wood, Polygon Wood, and Broodseinde Ridge.

31st Oct- The Australian Light Horse successfully charge Turkish positions, pushing the Allies through Turkish held Gaza.

Dec 20th - 2nd Conscription Referendum in Australia and forces overseas: – for 1,015,159, – against 1,181,747.

2nd Mar - Major General John Monash's 3rd Australian Division is sent south of Dernancourt to capture the village of Morlancourt.

26th Mar - Australian forces are sent to halt the German offensive in a series of battles starting at Hebuterne, south of Arras.

12th Apr - Australian forces defend the village of Hazebrouck, saving the strategic rail centre from destruction.

Apr 25th - Australian troops drive German forces from Villers-Bretonneux during a night attack, halting the German offensive.

Jun 1st -Lieutenant-General Sir John Monash is appointed to command the Australian Corps in France.

Jul 4th - Battle of Le Hamel. Major General Monash attacks with planes with bombs, tanks, heavy artillery and Lewis guns.

Aug 8th - Battle of Amiens. British and French troops, led by Australian and Canadian Corps, into German positions.

31st Aug - The 2nd Australian Division attacks and captures the hill of Mont St Quentin, the last German-held stronghold.

1st Oct - The 10th Australian Light Horsemen are the first Allied forces to enter Turkish-controlled Damascus.

Oct 5th - Australians take the village of Montbrehain, the last battle on the Western Front, breaking through the Hindenburg Line.

Nov 1st - Turkey surrenders and signs the Armistice with the Allies Servicemen start returning home via troopships.

28th Jun, Archduke Franz Ferdinand of the Austro-Hungarian Empire assassinated in Sarajevo.
1st Aug, Germany declares war on Russia. France mobilises its military forces.
4st Aug, Great Britain declares war on Germany!
Aug 23rd, German troops invade Poland. In one month, the world erupts in war!
23rd Sep, Allied forces join the Western Front.
5th Nov, Great Britain and France declare war on Turkey.
3rd Feb, Turkish forces occupy the Sinai.
7th May, German submarine the U20 sinks the Lusitania off the Irish coast, killing 1198 passengers and crew including 128 Americans.
29th Aug, Anzacs abandon further Gallipoli offensives and a stalemate begins.
11th Oct, Germans execute British nurse Edith Cavell, inspiring increased Allied war and recruitment efforts.
7th Dec, British troops evacuate Gallipoli.
21st Feb, The battle of Verdun begins on the Western Front.
25th April, First ANZAC Day commemerations for returning soldiers.
7th Jun, British Minister for War, Lord Kitchener, dies when the HMS Hampshire is sunk by a mine off the Orkney Islands.
15th Sep, Tanks are used for the first time in the Somme at Flers-Courcelette.
28th Oct, Conscription Referendum voted against!
18th Nov, The Somme campaign ends.
1st Feb, German forces on the Western Front withdraw to the Hindenburg Line — a stronger, reinforced defensive line.
6th April, The United States declares war on Germany.
25th Jun, The first American troops land in France.
28th Aug, The Allies advance on Flanders, Belgium.
1st Nov, German troops from the Eastern Front start arriving on the Western Front as Russia withdraws from the war.
9th Dec, Allied troops capture Jerusalem from Turkish forces.
3rd March, Russia and Germany sign the Treaty of Brest-Litovsk ending Russia's participation in the war.
23rd March, Paris is shelled from the Western Front.
21st April, Allied troops shoot down the Red Baron, top German pilot Manfred Albrecht Freiherr von Richthofen.
2nd Jul, Prime Minister William Morris Hughes addresses Australian troops on the Western Front before the battle of Le Hamel
3rd Nov, The former Austro-Hungarian Empire surrenders and signs the Armistice.
11th Nov, Germany signs the Armistice and fighting ceases on the Western Front.
